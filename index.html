Research Interests

Generally speaking, my research interests are natural language syntax, semantics and the interface of the two. My work is solidly within the framework of generative grammar, which seeks to explain how the innate structure of the mind gives rise to human language. Within my general interests, I have several specific interests both theoretical and empirical.

My primary empirical interest is the syntax and semantics of adpositional elements (i.e., prepositions, postpositions, etc.), especially Germanic particle verbs . In syntax, the the study of these elements raises questions of C/case, argument structure, and optionality. In semantics, adpositional phrases inform us of how the human mind represents physical space.

My main theoretical interest is in the formalization of the theory of syntax that has been developed within the minimalist program. In particular, I am interested in the conception of Agree as a syntacic operation and the consequences that conception has for derivational economy. Formalizing Agree also requires formalizing the representation of syntactic features.
